Skyrim | The Elder Scrolls V

Basic Knowledge Of The Game

Skyrim is a fantasy game, arguably based upon Norse Mythology and heritage, which starts you off as a prisoner on his way to an execution. You are in a cart with three other prisoners, and as you go towards the chopping block one of the prisoners explains the essence of the game and the reason each man is there. One of the other prisoners, Ulfric Stormcloak, is actually a Jarl of the Hold Windhelm, and will appear many times later in the game, in an optional mission to assist the Stormcloak rebellion against the Imperial legion. When you get down to the chopping block, you decide your character's name and race, both of which are completely up to you. The prisoner in front of you is executed, and then you are set to be next, but before you can be executed, a dragon attacks where you're being held, and you are able to escape. From there, the journey is yours to behold. From monsterous spiders in caves to bandit camps next to Holds, there's many enemies and adventures to explore and discover in Skyrim, and this website is to help you gain a foothold on your game. With nine holds, countless missions, endless activites, and many mods that can bring in additional features such as a whole new province named Solstheim, or smaller mods like adoption, Skyrim is an interactive, intriguing and quite frankly an addicting game, and I hope that this website will help you enjoy the game as much as I did.

The Nine Holds

In Skyrim, kingdoms and territories are marked by citadels named Holds. There are nine Holds in Skyrim;

  • Falkreath
  • Whiterun
  • Morthal
  • Dawnstar
  • Winterhold
  • Solitude
  • Riften
  • Markarth
  • Windhelm

Each of these holds will have either Stormcloak or Imperial guards, or maybe even High Elves. The guard type in a city can change depending on if you join the war and which side you take in it. Each hold will have a house they can give you, or in some holds (Hjaalmarch, Falkreath and The Pale) you can build a house, by purchasing land from the steward or Jarl of these holds. Each hold has a capital, which will be where you find the Jarl and steward and such.

Skyrim's initial release date was November 11th, 2011 for Windows, Xbox 360, and Playstation 3
The Dragonborn addition to the game wasn't actually in the first release of Skyrim, and was the final add-on by the developers for Skyrim
